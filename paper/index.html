    LM-MCVT: A Lightweight Multi-modal Multi-view Convolutional-Vision Transformer Approach for 3D Object Recognition

    2025 34th IEEE International Conference on Robot and Human Interactive Communication (RO-MAN )
    Songsong Xiong , and Kasaei , Hamidreza
    This paper proposes a Lightweight Multi-modal Multi-view Convolutional-Vision Transformer (LM-MCVT) network for 3D object recognition. The hybrid LM-MCVT architecture incorporates pre- and mid-level convolutional encoders along with local and global transformers to enhance feature extraction and recognition accuracy. Using the Globally Entropy-based Embeddings Fusion (GEEF) method, the model outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods on the synthetic ModelNet40 dataset and demonstrates superior performance on the real-world OmniObject3D dataset, validating its robustness across both synthetic and real-world 3D data.

    Lifelong Ensemble Learning based on Multiple Representations for Few-Shot Object Recognition

    Robotics and Autonomous Systems 174 (2024) 104615.
    Kasaei , Hamidreza^1 , and Songsong Xiong^1
    This paper presents a lifelong ensemble learning approach for few-shot object recognition in service robots. It combines deep representations and 3D shape descriptors with a memory unit for efficient object learning in open-ended scenarios. Experimental results show the method’s effectiveness in both offline and open-ended settings, demonstrating superior performance in lifelong learning tasks with few-shot samples.

    Enhancing Fine-Grained 3D Object Recognition Using Hybrid Multi-Modal Vision Transformer-CNN Models

    2023 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS)
    Songsong Xiong; Georgios Tziafas; Hamidreza Kasaei
    This paper proposes a hybrid multi-modal Vision Transformer (ViT) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) approach for fine-grained 3D object recognition in robotics. To address the lack of FGVC 3D datasets, two synthetic datasets were created$:$ one with 20 restaurant categories and another with 120 shoe instances. The hybrid model outperformed CNN-only and ViT-only baselines, achieving accuracy of 94.50% and 93.51%, respectively. The FGVC RGB-D datasets are made available for further research, and the method was demonstrated in both simulated and real-world robotic applications.

    Lane Detection and Trajectory Tracking Control of Autonomous Vehicle Based on Model Predictive Control

    International journal of automotive technology 21 (2020) 285-295.
    Jianjun Hu , Songsong Xiong , Junlin Zha & Chunyun Fu
    A novel method combining lane detection and model predictive control (MPC) enhances trajectory tracking for autonomous vehicles. Simulation results show a 19.35% reduction in tracking error, demonstrating improved robustness and adaptability.

    PROJECTS

    Project A: Lifelong Object Learning in Open-Ended Environments
    2021 â€“ 2022
    • Investigated the problem of continual object learning in open-ended environments, where robots must recognize unfamiliar objects with limited supervision and evolving object categories.
    • Proposed a hybrid multi-representation framework that integrates deep visual features with geometric 3D descriptors, combining the generalization capabilities of deep learning with spatial awareness from geometry.
    • Developed a dual-stream architecture with memory modules to support long-term storage and retrieval of learned object knowledge throughout the robot's operational lifetime.
    • Adopted an ask-and-teach interaction mechanism for incremental learning, and demonstrated the method's effectiveness through extensive evaluations on scalability, robustness, and real-world robotic experiments.
    Project B: Fine-Grained RGB-D Object Recognition
    2022 â€“ 2023
    • Addressed the problem of fine-grained 3D object recognition in service robotics, where inter-class similarity and intra-class variation pose significant challenges to conventional recognition models.
    • Proposed a multi-modality hybrid representation framework that jointly encodes RGB and depth information, utilizing transformer-based global context modeling and convolutional extraction of local geometric details.

    • Validated the proposed method on robotic platforms, demonstrating its applicability in real-world tasks and providing a dataset resource for subsequent research.
    Project C: Multi-View Multi-Modality for 3D Object Recognition
    2023 â€“ 2024
    • Addressed the challenge of object recognition under random and limited viewpoints, where reliance on single-view RGB input often leads to performance degradation due to incomplete or ambiguous features.
    • Proposed a hybrid multi-view, multi-modality recognition strategy, designed to fuse complementary information across views and sensing modalities for robust feature representation.
    • Developed a cascaded architecture combining convolutional and vision transformer modules to jointly capture global view-aware context and refine local discriminative details.
    • Conducted extensive evaluations across multiple public datasets, demonstrating consistent performance gains over existing state-of-the-art methods.
    Project D: Robot Grasping in Cluttered Environments
    2024 â€“ 2025
    • Investigated the challenge of parallel robotic grasping in cluttered environments, where stacked or partially occluded objects make perception and manipulation difficult for cost-effective solutions.

    • Designed the system to leverage the complementary strengths of CNNs, transformers, and Mamba, addressing their individual limitations and improving overall grasp representation quality.
    • Validated the approach through real-robot experiments, demonstrating adaptability and robustness across both isolated and occluded object scenarios.
